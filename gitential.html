Gitential

Gitential has been created to make software development measurable and enable data-driven improvements across your projects and teams by focusing on 4 pillars of Code Quality, Team Collaboration, Cost-Effective Project Deliveries and On-Time Software Releases.

Project Goals

  • Create and launch the MVP version of the product
  • Gather as much user feedback as possible, not just UX feedback but feedback on the product itself
  • Acquire the first paying customers

My Tasks

  • Interview with our early adopter users
  • Create user flows, wireframes for the MVP
  • Create and apply the design to the UI
  • Create the related pattern library
  • Gather user feedbacks and analytics and iterate on the design and features

Highlights

Dashboard

An effective analytical service offers users a comprehensive overview of their projects while also providing the option to access detailed information when desired. To achieve this balance, we have designed our dashboard to display only essential information and highlight key insights we deem most valuable.

For users who wish to delve deeper into their projects and the associated developers, the system is equipped to provide detailed data, advanced filtering options, and export functionalities. Additionally, our service includes customizable options, allowing users to tailor the dashboard and reports to their specific needs, ensuring a personalized and efficient analytical experience.

Data Visualization

One of the main challenges I faced was deciding how to present specific types of data and information. I had to consider the capabilities of our system, the functionalities offered by our data visualization framework, and whether the chosen visualizations effectively communicated the necessary insights.

To enhance user experience further, I incorporated customizable options, allowing users to add more charts with custom settings. This flexibility empowers users to personalize their dashboards, tailoring the experience to their individual preferences.

Onboarding

In response to user feedback, we identified the onboarding phase as one of the most challenging aspects, particularly when users needed to connect their repositories to our service. This process was complicated by varying requirements and security configurations across different services.

To address this challenge, our objective was to simplify the entire onboarding process. By minimizing the number of required steps and providing helpful guidance through helper texts, we aimed to decrease churn rates.

What have I learned?

It's important to listen the user feedbacks properly. Not just what UX problems do they have while using the site but fundamentally, what do they question or they don't understand about your product. It would have been better if we would communicate how do we mean under developer productivity and how do we measure it.

This is more like a product issue, but it's better to find a problem that needs to be solved first, rather than create a solution and trying to find the problem that it can solve later on.
