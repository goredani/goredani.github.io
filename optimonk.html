OptiMonk

OptiMonk is a free conversion optimization tool that turns your traffic into sales. It allows you to create, personalize and launch popups, sidebars, notification bars and fullscreens without coding. As a product designer, I was responsible for designing the new features and maintain, update the design system.

Product Goals

  • Create new and update existing features inside OptiMonk
  • Create landing pages for promotional and marketing purposes
  • User test new features to ensure our designs are usable and understandable for our users

My Tasks

  • Participating in product discovery sessions with the product team
  • Creating designs and interactive prototypes in Figma
  • Participating in user research and testing sessions
  • Maintaining and updating the component library in Figma
  • Gather user feedbacks and analytics and iterate on the design and features

Highlights

Discount Code sync with Shopify

One of the new features I worked on was the sync functionality between OptiMonk and Shopify. By linking their Shopify accounts to OptiMonk, users could create discount codes directly within the OptiMonk app, which would then automatically sync with Shopify.

My responsibilities included designing the user flow, creating the UI design, and developing an interactive prototype for demonstration and testing purposes.

Although the limitations of the Shopify API prevented us from achieving our initial vision for the user experience, we were still able to simplify the process for our users by enabling them to manage their discount codes within OptiMonk.

Launch Prototype

Advanced User Segmentation Tool

Originally, OptiMonk offered basic segmentation options, but some of our larger clients desired more sophisticated user segmentation capabilities. Our goal was to provide a comprehensive tool that allowed these clients to set up advanced segmentations with custom rules.

After gathering the available options, we developed an initial user flow for setting up segmentations. Through iterations with the product team and the use of interactive Figma prototypes, we achieved a solution that satisfied even the most unique segmentation requirements.

What went well?

  • User test sessions with real users provided valuable feedback during our solution testing.
  • Interactive, clickable prototypes are more effective for presenting solutions compared to static screens.

What didn't go well?

  • Product discovery sessions can be tiresome without a structured approach to presenting ideas.
  • We could have benefited from gathering more feedback on completed projects, which would have provided valuable insights about our products.

What have I learned?

Using structured frameworks, such as the Google Design Sprint, for discovery meetings provides faster, more organized feedback loops and encourages effective participation from all team members.
